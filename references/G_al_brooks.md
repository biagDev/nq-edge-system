# G. Al Brooks price action

## Summary
Al Brooks (psychiatrist-turned-trader, three-volume *Trading Price Action* series, Wiley 2011-2012; *Brooks Trading Course*) provides the most exhaustive published taxonomy of bar-by-bar price action. Key concepts: **signal bar** (the bar whose close triggers entry on the next bar's break — strong-close trend bar, doji, ii pattern), **MM bar** (measured-move continuation bar with a strong body and small tails), **two-legged pullback (M2B/M2S)** — Brooks' core trend-continuation entry, where after a thrust the market makes one corrective leg, a small bounce, a second corrective leg, then resumes — typically retesting the 20-EMA. The empirical claim: most pullbacks in healthy trends are two-legged because the first leg traps counter-trend traders, the bounce triggers their stops, and the second leg completes the test. **Failed-breakout / failed-failure** (his "high-1 / low-1" series) provides language for retest behaviors. Brooks reports practical win rates on his core setups in the 40-60% range with positive expectancy when sized correctly; he is unusually honest about realistic numbers compared to the rest of the price-action industry.

## What's evidence vs marketing
- **Evidence-grade**: Brooks' descriptive taxonomy is rigorous and well-defined enough to be coded. The two-legged-pullback observation is consistent with mean-reversion-of-pullbacks studies in trending regimes.
- **Important honest disclosure**: Brooks himself states strong trends often have only single-leg pullbacks, so insisting on M2B/M2S means *missing the strongest trends* — a real, named trade-off.
- **Where Brooks adds vs duplicates**: Brooks' bar-pattern vocabulary (signal bar, MM, M2B, follow-through bar) is genuinely useful as **execution-grade entry filters layered on top of a statistical edge**. They do NOT manufacture edge; they describe it. Good fit as a *trigger refinement* on top of D1/D2/B1 signals.
- **Marketing fluff**: There is no "Al Brooks system" that prints money. The course content is genuinely educational but is a vocabulary, not a turnkey strategy. Some derivative YouTube content overclaims.

## Verdict
Use Brooks' vocabulary as an **entry-trigger refinement** on top of statistical setups. E.g., for D1 (prior-day H/L retest), require the retest bar to be a signal bar (strong-close reversal candle) — this almost certainly reduces false retests at modest cost in trade count.
